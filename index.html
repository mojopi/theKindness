The Kindness





New York is as busy as busy can be
Fast-walking, fast-talking, a bustling city.








The veins of the city lie underground
Humming with music and loud squeaking sounds.

Subway trains pull into the stations.
Riders await with great hesitation.

Rushing to work and rushing to shows,
they’re rushing to eat, mingle, get some new clothes.

They’re rushing through skyscrapers and street theater,
Through museums, landmarks, phone-talkers, and readers.




New York is the city that never sleeps





But it went to bed, silent. There was not a peep.


A virus evolved that filled people with dread
It was very contagious, the doctors said.

Unlike adults, children didn’t get sick
no fevers nor coughs, asymptomatic








But when this small virus got inside the grown-ups
It hurt their lungs badly, so New York said “close up!”

To keep themselves safe, people distanced themselves.
To have food at home, they stocked their shelves.

They closed the schools and the shopping.
They said "Keep clean with soapy handwashing!"

This virus was tiny, ‘twas invisible
And it closed down a city that was invincible.
But in a small building; in three small rooms
Was a child, Cyan, and her mom, on “zoom.”

The mom worked from home, videoing all day long Cyan, home from school, was singing a song.

“Shhhh!” her mom hushed, “Can’t you see that I’m working!?”

Cyan was annoyed, stress was constantly lurking.

“Pillow fight?!” she requested. “I’m busy,” mom said,
“Cyan, clean your room,” she suggested instead.

“What about cookies?!” Cyan skipped about.
Over her screen, mom frowned with a pout.

She got the message: Cyan, go away!
So she went by herself to her room to play.

She flopped on her bed, feeling all alone.
She missed her friends, and not being home.

Moping, she whispered, “This new life is boring.”
A small voice responded, “Don’t mope, just get going!
